Who Are You?

Are you new cataloging books using your computer?

If you're new to cataloging applications such as Books, we'll explain how cataloging your books will help you manage your library.

Have you used other cataloging applications?

If you're comfortable cataloging books on your computer, we'll explain some of the features that distinguish Books from the competition.

Have you used earlier versions of Books?

If you are a former user of previous iterations of Books (prior to 3.x), we'll explain some of the changes and new features in Books 3.

Are you a current user of Books?

If you're a current user of Books 3 and feel like you have mastered the basics, we'll explain some helpful tips and tricks to improve your experience.

Are you interested in contributing to the Books project?

We're always looking for motivated people interested in lending a hand. If you're a potential developer, translator, or designer, we'll explain some of the ways that you can customize your own experience and contribute back to the project.

Are you interested in learning more about us?

We're a rag-tag group working hard to make the best book cataloging program for you. Learn a bit about us.
 

Common Topics of Interest

Using Scanners With Books
Books supports a variety of barcode scanners and cameras (including Apple's iSight) for scanning UPC codes.

Fetching Information Online
For all but the rarest items, Books can save you some typing by downloading information about your books from online sources like Amazon and the Library of Congress.

Lists & Smart Lists
We'll explain the differences between the two and help you make the most of both.

Exporting Your Collection
Do you want to use your data outside of Books? We'll explain how to do this.

Custom Fields
Do you want to add and use your own fields with Books? It's easy, but there are a few things that you should know.

Copies, Reviews, Lending History, & Files
